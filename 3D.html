    Natalie (size 4)

  • Bio - This was the first full sized mannequin I made using 3D scan of actual person. We used Go!3D hand held scanner made by creaform. The challenge was that the scanner only scans 15” x 15” and I needed to go around the body to get the whole scan. Eventually the model became tired and got harder for her to stay still. I was able to patch the broken scans after but I also learnt it’s important to scan fast and not to get greedy with the pose options. I also make sure to take all her measurements from head to toe. CLIENT - NORDSTROM, CHRISTOPHER & BANKS The process - It starts from ideation. The prefered pose and garment size is usually provided from the client. They can give us something vague first because I can always adjust the size & poses digitally. The body scan always speeds up my process. There are a few things I need to adjust to make it functional as a mannequin. So that’s also done at this stage. I usually send 360 renders for client’s shape approval. My employer owns rapid printers. We usually print 1:6 size of actual mannequin to send to a client. After that, we use CNC machine to produce a life size model so that the client can actually do a fit test. If they have any issues at that point, Our traditional sculptors are going to make the adjustment phsycally. Once the lifesize model gets an approval, I scan the change back into the computer, clean it up for the production and send the data to a mould maker.
